freenode bot monitoring

A project created to help fight the spy bot invasion on freenode.

Premise

On freenode there have been reports of bots joining channels, idling in them, and then quitting. It is unknown for what reason they do this, but they can be a nuiscance and should be banned. However, they constantly change IPs so this project was created to find the IPs.

Identifying features.

These are some identifying features of the bots.
  • Hostmask — These bots will have a cloak with their IP as the cloak. The ident will have a leading ~ and will follow with the first 9 letters of the nick. Additionally, the nick will be enitrely lower case with no numbers or any character besides the alphabet.
    Example: examplenick!~exmpleni@127.0.0.1
  • Quitting message — The bots will always quit after a short period with the message "Remote host has closed the connection."
  • CTCP — The bots will not reply to any form of CTCP, including CTCP PING. Do NOT CTCP them under any circumstances, even if you suspect that they may be a bot.
  • IP Range — THe bots have been comming from certain CIDR Ranges, see the next section for more information. Once the ranges are determined then you can ban those ranges.

    Rules

    1. Use common sense.
    2. Do not send notices to the channel.
    3. Do not bring unauthorized bots to the channel. This includes cllient scripts with automated triggers replying in-channel
    4. Do not query, CTCP, notice, or otherwise interact with any of the people that hexagon identifies as possible bots, as most of them are legit users.
